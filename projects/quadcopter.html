Overview

Designed and built a long-endurance quadcopter with a focus on maximizing flight time through careful component selection and tuning. The frame was 3D-printed, and every component from motor KV rating to propeller pitch was selected with hover efficiency as the primary metric.

The drone was equipped with an onboard analog FPV video link and went through iterative PID tuning to achieve stable, predictable flight with minimal oscillation — a prerequisite for genuine endurance flights.

Technical Highlights
  • 3D-printed frame optimized for component clearance and arm stiffness
  • Brushless motor and ESC selection based on thrust-efficiency curves for 21700 cell pack
  • Low-KV motor pairing with larger propellers to maximize hover efficiency
  • PID flight controller parameter tuning — rate and angle loops optimized for stability
  • Integrated onboard analog FPV video transmitter and camera
  • Achieved approximately 30 minutes of stable hover time in field tests
