Overview

Built a complete ray tracing rendering engine from scratch in C++, implementing all core components: geometry, shading, illumination, and recursive reflection. Every part of the pipeline was written without external graphics libraries.

The engine was profiled and optimized to render 4K scenes containing thousands of polygon vertices in under one minute on a standard CPU. It was also explored as a deterministic model for simulating RF and radio-wave propagation through physical environments.

Explored as a radio-wave propagation model — the deterministic ray-based approach maps naturally to how electromagnetic waves reflect and attenuate through materials.
Technical Highlights
  • Ray-sphere and ray-triangle geometric intersection with numerical precision handling
  • Recursive reflection rays with configurable maximum depth
  • Phong and Lambert shading models for specular and diffuse surfaces
  • Shadow ray casting for point and directional light sources
  • Distance attenuation for physically based light falloff
  • Full 4x4 homogeneous matrix transformation pipeline (translate, rotate, scale) enabling object-space intersection testing
  • Profiled and optimized ray-scene traversal to achieve 4K output in under one minute on CPU
